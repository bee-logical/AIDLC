# Design research — inspiration with evidence

Purpose: aim the pod at the *real, current* award bar and hand the design-system and motion agents
concrete techniques — not a memory of "good design" and not a mood board of pretty screenshots.

## Method

1. **Anchor to the narrative.** Research serves this surface's tone and signature moment. A
   reference is relevant only if it informs a specific beat.
2. **Find real, recent work.** Awwwards (Site of the Day / nominees), FWA, respected studio
   portfolios, current showcases. Recent matters — award standards shift yearly. Use WebSearch to
   locate and WebFetch to inspect the actual pages and their write-ups.
3. **Extract transferable technique, not vibes.** For each reference, capture what is reusable:
   grid/layout, type pairing and scale, color/gradient construction, and especially motion —
   triggers, easing character, sequencing, scroll behavior. Note what wouldn't fit our tone.
4. **Distill.** Collapse the references into 3–5 synthesized directions stated concretely enough to
   act on (e.g. "scroll-linked opacity+translate reveals, 200–400ms ease-out; oversized editorial
   display type over monospaced meta labels; 8pt-multiple whitespace with a single accent gradient").
5. **Trend guardrails.** Note what's currently winning vs. what already reads dated — each claim tied
   to where you saw it.

## Rules

- Every reference cites a real, reachable URL. No invented examples; no uncited trend assertions.
- Inspiration is not imitation. Extract principles; never propose cloning a specific site.
- Stay in service of the narrative — breadth for its own sake is noise.

## Output

`design/inspiration.md` from the template: references with transferable techniques, distilled
directions, trend guardrails.
